Our Technology

Our state-of-the-art, on-site technology allows us to provide customers with high-quality prescription eyewear using the most innovative imaging, testing and edging equipment available. We pride ourselves on providing leading eye and vision care with the most up-to-date technology at our optometry clinic in Burlington.

Eyewear Edging Technology

Exceptional service requires exceptional tools. We use advanced equipment to provide on-site edging, decreasing turnaround time and ensuring you receive your eyewear quickly.

How It Works

Our computerized and automated edging system, paired with our in-house optical lab, allows lenses to be made efficiently — so clients who need their orders expedited can count on our combination of equipment and expertise to get the job done quickly and correctly.

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)

OCT is a noninvasive imaging test used to take high-resolution, cross-sectional images of the retina. The retina has many distinctive layers that can be measured and evaluated from OCT images, helping us detect and diagnose many different retinal diseases and conditions.

OCT uses rays of light — not x-rays or radiation — to measure retinal thickness, making it a non-invasive, comfortable test. Our Topcon Maestro 2 OCT allows us to conduct this imaging and testing on-site within minutes.

Visual Field Testing

A visual field test measures your entire scope of vision, including central and peripheral vision — how much each eye can see individually, and whether any blind spots or subtle areas of vision loss are present.

Visual field testing helps detect signs of glaucoma, which can damage the optic nerve, along with central or peripheral retinal diseases and conditions affecting the visual pathways from the optic nerve to the brain. The test is quick, easy and completely painless. We use an innovative Heidelberg Edge Perimeter (HEP) machine on-site to conduct this test in minutes.

Retinal Imaging

Digital retinal photography allows us to detect, diagnose and monitor eye diseases with precision and accuracy, taking a digital photo of the back of your eye to show the retina, optic disc and blood vessels. A close look at these internal structures ensures we have the information necessary to pinpoint early signs of disease.

Retinal imaging is non-invasive and typically takes under 5 minutes, though we may need to dilate your pupils first (up to 20 minutes to take effect). Your vision may be blurry for a few hours afterward, but the images are ready to view right away and your optometrist will discuss the findings with you before you leave.
