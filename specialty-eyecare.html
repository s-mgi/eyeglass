Burlington's eyecare specialists — at Eyeglass Gallery we pride ourselves on providing quality vision care options for customers of all ages, from regular eye examinations to specialty eyecare solutions.

Eye Exams

Pediatric Eye Exams

The Canadian Association of Optometrists recommends that children have at least one eye exam before starting school, and annually thereafter. Children are often unaware of what their vision should be, and classroom learning is largely visual — a comprehensive exam is essential to assess vision and eye health early.

Adult Eye Exams

Annual comprehensive eye exams are important for the assessment and early detection of common eye diseases, many of which show no obvious symptoms until advanced and difficult to treat. Dr. Chavoustie uses the latest technology to aid in early diagnosis and treatment.

Diabetic Eye Exams

People with diabetes have a higher risk of developing eye disease. In Ontario, OHIP covers annual eye exams for people with diabetes when performed by an optometrist or ophthalmologist, and exams can be booked without a referral.

Dry Eye Therapy

Dry eye disease is an uncomfortable condition that results from inadequate tear production and tear instability. It can be caused by medication side effects, autoimmune conditions, long-term contact lens wear, hormonal changes and extended screen use. Regular comprehensive eye exams are an excellent way of identifying signs of dry eye disease early.

Eye Disease Diagnosis & Management

Most common eye diseases are asymptomatic in their early stages, so having your eyes checked regularly is essential to maintaining good ocular health.

Glaucoma

An eye disease that can cause permanent vision loss and often goes unnoticed until it's too late. It occurs when elevated intraocular pressure damages the optic nerve — this can be detected on routine eye exams and managed to prevent further vision loss.

Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)

A degenerative eye disease affecting the macula, the part of the retina responsible for detailed central vision. AMD can significantly impact and impair central vision.

Cataracts

A natural part of aging that causes the eye's naturally clear lens to cloud over, often without obvious symptoms at first. UV protection can help slow the progression, and advanced cases may require surgery to remove them.

Digital Eye Strain

If you regularly use digital devices like smartphones, tablets or computers, you may experience digital eye strain — dry, tired eyes after prolonged use. How long you spend on screens, your distance from them, your workstation setup and lighting can all affect eye comfort.

Many optometrists recommend the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, take a 20-second break to focus on something 20 feet away. Blue light lenses are also available in store to help combat digital eye strain.
