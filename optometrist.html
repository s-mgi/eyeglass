Dr. Matt Chavoustie · OD

Education & training

Dr. Chavoustie completed his undergraduate studies at the State University of New York at Oswego in 2004, receiving a Bachelor's Degree in Biological Sciences. He went on to earn his Doctor of Optometry degree from the prestigious New England College of Optometry in Boston in 2009 — where he also met his wife, fellow optometrist Dr. Anjali Pathak.

During his final-year rotations, Dr. Chavoustie trained coast to coast across a wide variety of clinical settings, focusing on fitting specialty contact lenses and co-managing LASIK patients in Atlanta, Georgia. Following graduation, he was accepted into the Ocular Disease Residency Program at the Boston Veteran's Affairs Hospital System, working closely with globally-renowned specialists in glaucoma, retinal and corneal disease.

He has managed complex ocular diseases — including glaucoma, macular degeneration and diabetic retinopathy — at VA Hospitals in New York's Hudson Valley and Portland, and cared for an under-served community at Boston's Upham's Corner Community Health Center. He has also mentored 3rd- and 4th-year optometry students and handled urgent cases referred from hospital emergency rooms.

When not in the office, Dr. Chavoustie enjoys exploring all that Burlington offers with his wife and their two young children.
